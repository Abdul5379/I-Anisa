Frequently Asked Questions

How does image resizing work?

Image resizing works by changing the number of pixels in an image to match your desired dimensions. Our tool uses high-quality algorithms to ensure the resized image maintains as much detail and clarity as possible while changing to your specified dimensions.

Will resizing affect the quality of my images?

When you resize an image, some quality loss is inevitable, especially when enlarging images. However, our tool uses advanced algorithms to minimize quality loss. Downscaling (making images smaller) generally maintains better quality than upscaling (making images larger).

What does "maintain aspect ratio" mean?

Aspect ratio is the proportional relationship between an image's width and height. When you check "maintain aspect ratio" and change either the width or height, our tool automatically calculates the other dimension to keep the original proportions. This prevents your image from looking stretched or squished.

What image formats are supported?

Our tool supports the most common web image formats: JPEG/JPG, PNG, SVG, and GIF. Each format is resized using format-specific techniques to ensure optimal results.

Is there a file size limit?

Since all processing happens in your browser, the file size limit depends on your device's memory. Generally, files up to 20MB should work fine on most devices. For very large images, you might experience slower performance.

Is my data secure when using this tool?

Yes, your images are processed entirely in your browser and are never uploaded to our servers. This ensures complete privacy and security of your images.

Why would I need to resize my images?

There are many reasons to resize images: to fit specific dimensions for websites or social media platforms, to reduce file size for faster loading, to prepare images for printing, or to create thumbnails. Proper image sizing is essential for optimal performance and appearance across different devices and platforms.
